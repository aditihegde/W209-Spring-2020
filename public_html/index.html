Impact of Climate Change

The Greenhouse Effect on Overdrive

The Greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth's surface. When the sun's energy reaches the atmosphere some is reflected back into space and the rest is absorbed and is re-radiated by greenhouse gases. This absorbed energy warms the atmosphere and the surface of the Earth making life possible. Human activity has increased the amount of greenhouse gases released into the atmosphere. This is trapping excess heat in the atmosphere and causing the Earth's temperature to increase and putting the Greenhouse Effect on Overdrive.


Greenhouse effect steps:

  • Step 1: Solar radiation reaches the Earth's atmosphere - some of this is reflected back into space.
  • Step 2: The rest of the sun's energy is absorbed by the land and the oceans, heating the Earth.
  • Step 3: Heat radiates from Earth towards space.
  • Step 4: Some of this heat is trapped by greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, keeping the Earth warm enough to sustain life.
  • Step 5: Human activities such as burning fossil fuels, agriculture and land clearing are increasing the amount of greenhouse gases released into the atmosphere.
  • Step 6: This is trapping extra heat, and causing the Earth's temperature to rise.
